The Process

Simpler Than
You Think.

Three steps. Your only job is to remove the hardware and show up twice — once to drop off, once to pick up. Everything in between is ours.

Step 01

Your Job

Remove & Drop Off

Take every door and drawer face off the cabinet boxes. Strip all hardware from each piece. Give them a basic wipe-down. Load them in your vehicle along with the 2K coating you purchased, and bring them to our shop. That is the complete list of what you need to do before drop-off.

We do not need you to sand anything. We do not need the doors pre-cleaned beyond a basic wipe. We do not need you to fill holes or address surface damage — we'll assess that at drop-off. Your job is to remove, strip hardware, and transport.

Leave the cabinet boxes in your kitchen. We only refinish the door panels and drawer faces you bring to us. The boxes stay in place the entire time.

Hardware Removal — Non-Negotiable

Every hinge, pull, knob, handle, and glass insert must be removed before drop-off. A door that arrives with hardware attached will either be returned or have a removal charge added. There is no workaround — we cannot spray over hardware.

What To Bring

Your doors and drawer faces, fully stripped of hardware. The sealed, unmixed 2K coating we specified in your quote. That is everything. We handle the rest from that point forward.

Vinyl-Wrapped Doors

If any of your doors have thermofoil or vinyl wrap, tell us when you book. They're handled differently and priced at a separate tier. Don't sand them or try to remove the wrap yourself — we strip it as part of our process.

$0 Due At Drop-Off

You do not pay anything when you drop off your doors. Nothing is due until you pick up and approve the work. Drop-off is just the handoff — the financial transaction happens at the end.

Step 02

Our Job

We Handle Everything

From the moment your doors are in our shop, the process is entirely ours to manage. Sanding, prep, primer, spray application, cure time — every step happens here, on our timeline, to our standard.

Most customers have no idea how much happens between drop-off and the finished product. The spray itself is a relatively small portion of the total work. What takes time — and what determines whether the finish lasts 2 years or 20 — is everything that happens before the gun comes out.

Turnaround is typically about one week from drop-off, depending on current workload. We'll give you an honest estimate when you book, and we'll reach out when your doors are ready rather than making you check in repeatedly.

Inspection At Drop-Off

Every piece gets assessed when you arrive. We'll note condition issues, flag anything that qualifies for the deep clean add-on, identify vinyl-wrapped pieces, and confirm the complete job scope before you leave.

Full Sanding & Prep

Complete sanding of all surfaces prior to coating. Not a light scuff — a proper prep pass that creates the mechanical adhesion the coating needs to bond and hold long-term.

Tack & Final Clean

Immediately before spraying, every surface is tack-cleaned to remove any dust or contamination from the sanding process. This step happens right before the gun comes out — not hours earlier.

HVLP Spray Application

Applied through a professional turbine HVLP system. Multiple coats with proper flash time between each. The coating is your 2K catalyzed material — the same spec we provided, mixed to ratio, applied correctly.

Cure Time

2K coatings cure chemically. We do not release doors before they've reached proper hardness. A soft finish on pickup is a failure — we don't let that happen by rushing the cure.

Pre-Release Inspection

We review every piece before contacting you for pickup. Anything that doesn't meet standard gets addressed before it leaves the shop, not after you've hung it back up and called us about it.

Step 03

The Finish Line

Pick Up & Pay

We reach out when your doors are ready. You come in, look at every piece, and pay when you're satisfied. Not before. Then you take them home, hang them back up, reattach your hardware, and you have a kitchen that looks like a different room.

The reinstallation is the same process you did in reverse — hang the doors, attach the hardware, and you're done. Most customers are surprised by how straightforward the whole thing is once they've been through it. The prep work felt like a lot going in. The pickup feels like a transformation.

If for any reason something isn't right, we have that conversation at pickup before money changes hands. In practice that conversation hasn't been necessary — but the offer stands regardless.

We Contact You

You don't need to follow up to check on your job. We reach out when the doors are done, inspected, and ready for pickup. Typically around the one-week mark from drop-off.

Inspect Everything

Look at every door and drawer face before you load them up. Ask questions. Look at the finish from different angles. That inspection is the point — not a formality.

Pay At Pickup

Cash, check, Venmo, Zelle, or card. We'll confirm the method when you book. The total matches the quote we gave you at the outset, plus any add-ons confirmed at drop-off.

Keep The Leftover Coating

The coating you purchased is yours. We use what the job requires and return the remainder. That leftover is your touch-up supply for years to come — same material, exact match.

Inside The Shop

What Actually Happens
Between Drop-Off
And Pickup.

Most people think spray finishing is mostly spraying. It isn't. Here's the actual sequence every job goes through.

Day 1

Intake & Assessment

Every piece logged and inspected. Condition noted. Any add-ons confirmed. Job entered into the queue.

Prep Phase

Full Sanding

All surfaces sanded for mechanical adhesion. The existing finish is scuffed to give the new coating something to bond to. This is the most time-consuming part of the job and the part most shortcuts skip.

If Needed

Primer Coats

Where substrate condition or surface type requires it — vinyl-stripped MDF, heavily repaired surfaces — primer coats are applied and allowed to fully dry before topcoat work begins.

Spray Day

Tack, Mix, Spray

Final tack cleaning. Coating mixed to ratio. Applied through the HVLP system in multiple coats with proper flash time between each. Environment controlled for dust and temperature.

Cure Phase

Full Chemical Cure

2K coatings cure by chemical reaction, not just evaporation. This takes time. We don't rush it. Doors stay in the shop until the coating has reached proper hardness — not just surface-dry.

Before Pickup

Inspection & Release

Every piece reviewed before we contact you. Anything that doesn't pass gets addressed here, before it leaves the shop. Then we reach out and get you scheduled for pickup.

Before You Arrive

Your Complete
Drop-Off Checklist

This is everything you need to have done before bringing your doors to us. Nothing more, nothing less.

Your Responsibility
  • Remove all doors and drawer faces from cabinet boxes
  • Pull every hinge, pull, knob, handle, and glass insert from each piece
  • Wipe surfaces down — loose dust and light debris only
  • Contact us to confirm we have capacity and get your drop-off scheduled
  • Purchase the 2K coating we specified — bring it sealed and unmixed
  • Note any vinyl or thermofoil wrapped pieces when you book
  • Transport doors to our Hickory shop on your scheduled drop-off date
We Handle From Here
  • All sanding and surface preparation
  • Final tack cleaning before spray
  • Primer coats where required by substrate or condition
  • Coating mix ratios and pot life management
  • HVLP spray application and coat sequencing
  • Flash time and cure time management
  • Pre-release inspection of every piece
  • Vinyl stripping on vinyl-tier doors
