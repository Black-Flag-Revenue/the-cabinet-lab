Put It In Perspective

A Typical Kitchen.
What You're Actually
Comparing Against.

Ten standard doors and six drawer faces is a fairly common kitchen. At Cabinet Door Lab, that job runs $1,100 in labor — plus whatever you spend on the coating you purchase directly, typically $150 to $300 depending on quantity and color.

An in-home cabinet painter in this area will quote you somewhere between $1,500 and $4,000 for the same kitchen — and apply brush-and-roll latex that starts showing wear in two to three years in a busy kitchen environment.

Full cabinet replacement? You're looking at $5,000 on the low end and $20,000 or more if you want quality materials. And you still end up with a finish that came out of a production line, not a professional spray booth.

What's In The Price

Every Line Item
Includes This.

The per-piece price covers the complete finishing process — not just the spray time. There's a significant amount of prep work that goes into a finish before the gun comes out, and all of it is included.

The one thing not included is the coating itself — because you purchase it directly. We give you the exact spec and quantity. You buy it at retail with no markup from us.

  • Complete sanding of all surfaces prior to coating
  • Surface prep and degreasing to standard kitchen condition
  • Tack cleaning immediately before spray — no dust contamination
  • Primer coats where the substrate or condition requires it
  • Professional HVLP spray application — multiple coats to spec
  • Proper flash time between coats
  • Full cure time before release — no soft finish on pickup
  • Visual inspection of every piece before we contact you for pickup
