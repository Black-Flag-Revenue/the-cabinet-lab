Who's Holding The Gun

Built Around The Work
That Always Mattered Most.

For years I ran a construction and fine finish company alongside a partner. We covered the full range — framing, builds, interiors — but the finishing work was always what I took most seriously. Spray finishing in particular. When you spend enough time behind a gun, you develop a standard for what a surface should look and feel like. That standard makes it difficult to accept anything less.

A bad hernia — one that still gives me trouble — and some kidney health challenges changed what I could sustain physically. The heavy lifting, the extended site days, the full grind of construction work had to stop. The spray finishing stayed. That's where Cabinet Door Lab came from.

The Full Story

There's a specific feeling you get when a finish comes out exactly right. The surface is flat, the sheen is even across the entire piece, you run your hand across it and feel nothing because there is nothing to feel. That's what a properly sprayed catalyzed coating looks like when everything — the prep, the environment, the material, the application — is dialed in. I chased that feeling for years doing fine finish work, and I never got tired of it.

When I was running the construction company, finishing was always the department I held the tightest. Most contractors treat finish work as the thing you rush through at the end. I treated it as the thing the rest of the job was building toward. You can frame a house in days. A good finish is what people actually live with.

Spray finishing has always been
the part of this trade I cared most about.
Nothing changed.

The hernia came on gradually, then became impossible to work around. Between that and navigating some kidney health issues, the physical demands of full construction work weren't sustainable anymore. What I could do was stand behind a spray setup in a shop environment, manage a controlled process, and produce a finish that meets the standard I've held for years. That's not a consolation prize. That's the part of the work I actually wanted to do.

Cabinet Door Lab is the result of that calculus. A focused, single-service shop that does one thing — spray cabinet doors with a professional 2K catalyzed coating — at a level the market in this area doesn't consistently deliver. Every door that comes through here gets handled by one person. The same person who answers your text, assesses your doors at drop-off, sands them, sprays them, and hands them back to you at pickup. There is no crew. There is no variance. There is one finisher who cares about the outcome, working on your doors from start to finish.

That's not something you get from a franchise or a painting company that decided cabinets were a good upsell. It's what you get when the person running the shop has spent years caring about this specific kind of work.

What We Hold To

The Cabinet Door Lab
Standard

01

One Service. Done Properly.

We spray cabinet doors. That's it. No upsells into painting your walls, no add-on services that dilute the focus. Every system, every piece of equipment, every part of the workflow here is optimized for one job. Cabinet doors, sprayed properly, with the right material.

02

The Right Coating, Not The Easy One

Latex is easy. It's available everywhere, easy to apply, and easy to sell to someone who doesn't know what their finish actually is. A catalyzed 2K polyurethane is harder to work with and requires real spray equipment to apply correctly. It also lasts three to four times longer and performs at a level latex cannot reach. We use it because it's the right material for the job, not because it's convenient.

03

Prep Is The Finish

Most of what determines the quality of a spray finish happens before the gun comes out. Sanding, surface prep, tack cleaning — the unsexy work that separates a finish that lasts from one that peels in two years. We don't skip it. Every piece gets the full prep sequence before a drop of coating touches it.

04

You Know What You're Paying For

You purchase the coating directly. You see the price, you keep the leftover, you know exactly what went on your doors. We don't mark up materials or bundle them into a total that obscures what you're actually paying for labor versus product. Flat rate per piece. Pay at pickup. Nothing hidden.

05

Pay When You're Satisfied

Nothing is due until you pick up your doors and look at the work. That's not a promotional offer — it's how a craftsman-run shop operates. If the finish doesn't meet the standard, we talk about it before money changes hands. In practice, that conversation has never been necessary.

06

Local. Accountable. Accessible.

This is a local business serving the Hickory NC area — Statesville, Mooresville, Charlotte, the Lake Norman corridor. When you text the number on this site, you reach the person who will be spraying your doors. Not a call center. Not a booking platform. One finisher running one focused shop.
