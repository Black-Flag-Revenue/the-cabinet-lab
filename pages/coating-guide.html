You buy the coating. We spray it. Here's everything you need to know about what to buy, why 2K matters, and what makes our finish different from a paint job.

Why You Supply The Coating

No Markup. Ever.

Most finishing shops charge a massive markup on materials. They buy the coating at contractor pricing and charge you two to three times that in the final invoice — and you never know what you actually paid for the product itself.

We operate differently. You purchase the coating directly from a paint supplier or online at retail or near-retail cost. You bring it with your doors. We spray it. You pay only for the labor and expertise.

This means you get to see exactly what goes on your doors, you have the leftover coating for touch-ups later, and you're not paying a mystery contractor markup on the most important part of the job.

What Is 2K Coating?

Two Parts. One Remarkable Finish.

2K stands for two-component — a base coating and a hardener/catalyst that are mixed immediately before use. Once mixed, a chemical reaction begins that doesn't stop until the coating is fully cured to a rock-hard, durable film. This is fundamentally different from standard paint.

🧪

Chemical Cure

Standard paints cure by evaporation — the solvent leaves and what's left is a film. 2K coatings cure by a chemical cross-linking reaction between the resin and isocyanate hardener. The result is a polymer matrix that's far harder and more resistant than evaporative films.

💎

True Hardness

Cured 2K polyurethane achieves a hardness comparable to automotive clear coat. It resists scratching, marring, chipping, and impact far better than any latex or water-based cabinet paint — even "cabinet-specific" formulas at the hardware store.

🛡️

Chemical Resistance

Kitchen cabinets get hit with grease, cleaning products, steam, and spills daily. 2K coatings resist all of it. Latex paint — even the expensive ones — will eventually show wear, yellowing, and water damage. 2K won't.

How To Purchase

Step-By-Step:
Buying Your Coating

1

Contact Us First

Before you buy anything, text or email us. Tell us how many pieces you have, what color you want, and the sheen level (matte, satin, semi-gloss, gloss). We'll tell you the exact product line to purchase and the quantity needed for your job.

2

Choose Your Color

Most people refinish in white, off-white, or a neutral gray — colors that make a kitchen feel fresh and modern. We can tint the coating to a specific color. If you have a color code from another paint brand, bring that to the supplier and ask them to match it in the 2K base.

3

Buy At A Paint Supply Store

Not a hardware store — a dedicated paint supply house. Think Sherwin-Williams contractor locations, local independent paint stores, or automotive refinishing suppliers. These locations stock professional 2K products. Big-box hardware stores generally do not carry true 2K catalyzed cabinet coatings.

4

Get The Kit

2K products come as a kit — base and hardener sold together or separately. Follow the ratio on the label (usually 4:1 or 3:1 base to hardener by volume). Buy enough for your job plus some extra — we'll give you the quantity recommendation when you quote.

5

Don't Open It

Bring the coating to us in sealed, unmixed containers. We handle the mixing ratios and pot life management. Opening and partially mixing it before drop-off will waste product and could compromise the finish.
