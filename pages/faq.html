01

The fundamental questions about what this service is and how it works.

The Basics

It's a refinishing service where you handle the removal and transportation, and we handle the finishing. You take your cabinet doors and drawer faces off, strip all hardware, and bring them to our shop in Hickory NC along with the 2K coating we specify. We sand, prep, and spray every piece with a professional turbine HVLP system. You pick them up about a week later, inspect the work, and pay at pickup. No contractor in your home. No overspray in your kitchen. No scheduling around your household for days while someone brushes latex on your cabinets.
We spray a catalyzed 2K polyurethane coating — a two-component system where a base and hardener are mixed immediately before application and cure by chemical reaction rather than evaporation. The result is a film significantly harder and more durable than any single-component paint. It resists moisture, grease, scratching, and cleaning products at a level that latex or oil-based cabinet paint simply cannot match. This is the same class of coating used in production cabinet factories. See our 2K Coating Guide for the full breakdown.
No. We only refinish the door panels and drawer faces you bring to us. The cabinet boxes stay in your kitchen throughout the entire process. Most customers find the boxes look fine once the doors come back finished — the doors are what you see. If you want the boxes sprayed, that's in-home work we don't offer.
For the finish quality, yes — significantly. In-home cabinet painters brush or roll latex paint. The finish looks painted because it is. We spray a catalyzed 2K coating through a professional HVLP system in a controlled shop environment. The result is flat, smooth, and hard in a way that brush application cannot produce regardless of how skilled the painter is. On top of that, there's no mess in your home, no fumes in your kitchen, and no contractor access to your house for multiple days. You also pay considerably less for a better result.
One person. The same person who answers your texts, assesses your doors at drop-off, does the sanding and prep, sprays the coating, and hands the doors back to you at pickup. There's no crew, no subcontractor, no variance in who's working on your job. Cabinet Door Lab is a single-operator shop run by a finisher with years of construction and fine finish background. See the About page for the full story.

What you need to do before showing up, and what happens if you don't.

Preparation

Yes. Every hinge, pull, knob, handle, and glass insert must be removed before drop-off — no exceptions. We cannot spray over hardware. A door that arrives with hinges still attached will either be returned or have a removal charge added. This is the one non-negotiable requirement on your end, and it's a reasonable one. The hardware removal is straightforward with a basic screwdriver and takes most people an hour or two for a full kitchen.

We handle the real prep. The only exception is heavy grease or kitchen exhaust contamination that goes well beyond normal use — if that's present on some pieces, a $15 per piece deep clean add-on may apply, assessed when you drop off. We'll tell you which pieces qualify before any work begins.

Don't try to remove the wrap yourself before bringing them — we strip it as part of our process. Tell us upfront when you book that you have vinyl-wrapped pieces so we quote the correct tier.

05

What the finish looks like, how it holds up, and what to expect long-term.

The Finish

Significantly more durable. A properly applied 2K catalyzed coating achieves a hardness level — comparable to automotive clear coat — that latex paint cannot reach regardless of how many coats are applied. It resists scratching, marring, moisture, grease, and cleaning products at a level latex simply cannot match. In a busy kitchen, the best latex cabinet paints typically show noticeable wear within two to five years. A 2K finish, properly applied, should hold up for 10 to 20+ years under the same conditions.
Significantly less than oil-based paints, which yellow noticeably within a few years — especially on white or light colors. 2K polyurethane is among the more color-stable coating types available for cabinetry. Some yellowing can occur over a very long period in high-UV environments, but it's minimal compared to any oil-based alternative and better than most latex options in kitchen conditions.
Yes. The 2K coating can be tinted to a wide range of colors. Most customers choose white, off-white, or soft neutrals — the standard choices for a kitchen refresh that holds up long-term. If you have a specific color in mind, tell us when you get your quote. If you have a color code from another paint system, bring that to the paint supplier when you purchase the coating and ask them to match it in the 2K base.
Matte, satin, semi-gloss, and gloss depending on the product line. Satin is the most popular choice for kitchen cabinets — easy to clean, doesn't show fingerprints badly, clean and professional without being flashy. We'll confirm available sheens when we specify your coating.
That's one of the advantages of you owning the coating. The leftover 2K material from your job is an exact match to the finish on your doors. Store it properly — sealed, away from temperature extremes — and it will be usable for touch-ups for an extended period. We'll give you guidance on storage and shelf life when you pick up.
