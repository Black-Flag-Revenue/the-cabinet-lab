What To Tell Us

Everything We Need
To Quote Your Job.

The more detail you give us upfront, the faster we can get you an accurate number. Here's what we need.

01

Door & Drawer Count

How many standard cabinet doors, drawer faces, and pantry or oversized doors you have. An exact count gets you an exact quote. A rough count gets you a ballpark — which is fine if you're just exploring.

02

Surface Type

Are any of your doors or drawer faces wrapped in vinyl or thermofoil? These go on the vinyl-coated tier and are priced differently. If you're not sure, text us a photo — we can tell from a picture.

03

Color Preference

White, off-white, gray, navy, or something else? If you have a specific paint color in mind from another manufacturer, note the color name or code and we'll advise on matching it in the 2K system.

04

Your General Location

City or area you're coming from. Helps us confirm you're in our drop-off range and gives us context for scheduling. If you're coming from Charlotte or further, we can flag anything worth knowing before you make the drive.

After You Reach Out

Here's Exactly
What Happens Next.

01

We Quote You

Based on your door count and types, we send back a flat-rate total. No range. No "starting from." A specific number you can plan around.

02

We Specify The Coating

We tell you exactly what 2K product to purchase, where to get it, and how much to buy for your job. You pick it up at a paint supply store — sealed, unmixed, ready to bring to drop-off.

03

You Schedule A Drop-Off

Once you have your coating and your doors are stripped of hardware, we confirm a drop-off time. We give you the address and anything else you need to know before you arrive.

04

We Do The Work

Full sanding, prep, spray, and cure. Approximately one week. We reach out when your doors are ready — you don't need to follow up.

05

You Pick Up And Pay

Come in, look at every piece, pay when you're satisfied. Take your doors home, hang them back up, and you have a kitchen that looks entirely different. That's the whole process.
