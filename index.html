01

Catalyzed 2K Coating

Two-component polyurethane that cures by chemical reaction — not evaporation. The finished film is harder than any latex or single-component paint, resists moisture and grease, and holds its color for a decade or more. This is what production cabinet shops spray on new doors before they ship to dealers.

02

Turbine HVLP Application

High-volume low-pressure atomization produces a spray pattern that lays flat and smooth at a level a brush or roller physically cannot achieve. No lap marks. No texture. No build-up in corners. The kind of finish you run your hand across and feel nothing — because there's nothing to feel.

The Material

Why 2K Coating
Is In A Different
Class Entirely.

The cabinet painting industry runs on latex. It's easy to buy, easy to apply with a brush, and easy to sell to homeowners who don't know what their finish actually is. The problem is that latex paint — even expensive "cabinet-specific" formulas — is a soft film that wears, yellows, and peels under real kitchen conditions within a few years.

A catalyzed 2K polyurethane cures by a chemical cross-linking reaction that produces a polymer matrix harder than what any single-component paint can achieve. It resists the daily abuse of a working kitchen — grease, steam, cleaning products, impact — at a level that latex simply cannot match.

We spray it through a professional turbine HVLP system that atomizes the material into a flat, even film. The result is a finish that looks and performs like what came out of the factory — because that's the same class of material and the same method of application.

Full Story

I spent years running a construction and fine finish company. The details that most contractors don't slow down for — trim work, millwork, spray finishing — that was the work I actually cared about. When you spend enough time in fine finish, you develop a standard for what a surface should look and feel like, and it becomes impossible to accept anything less.

A bad hernia and some health challenges forced me off the heavier physical side of contracting. The spray work stayed. Cabinet Door Lab is how I stay in the craft — in a shop, focused, doing the one part of the trade I've always been most serious about.

Every door that comes through here gets sprayed by one person. The same person who answers your texts and assesses your doors at drop-off. There's no crew, no subcontractor, no variance in who's holding the gun from job to job. Just a finisher who gives a damn about the outcome.
