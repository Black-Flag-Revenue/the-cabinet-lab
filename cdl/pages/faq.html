The Coating & Finish
2K means two-component — a base and a hardener/catalyst mixed together immediately before use. Once mixed, a chemical reaction cures the coating to a film that's harder and more durable than anything that evaporation-cures (like latex paint). It's the same class of coating used by cabinet manufacturers to finish products in their factories. See our full Coating Guide for more.
Significantly more durable. Cured 2K polyurethane resists scratching, marring, moisture, grease, and common cleaning products at a level that latex cabinet paint cannot match. A well-applied 2K finish on cabinets can last 15–20+ years. The best latex cabinet paints typically need touch-up or recoat within 3–5 years in a busy kitchen.
Yes. We can tint the 2K coating to a wide range of colors. Most people choose white, off-white, or soft neutrals — classic and timeless for kitchens. If you have a specific color in mind, tell us when you quote and we'll advise on matching or tinting options.
Matte, satin, semi-gloss, and gloss — depending on the product line we're working with. Satin is the most popular for cabinets. It's easy to clean, doesn't show fingerprints badly, and looks clean without being flashy.
2K polyurethane is significantly more resistant to yellowing than oil-based paints (which yellow noticeably) and better than most latex formulas. Lighter colors in high-UV environments may show some shift over many years, but this is minimal compared to other coating types.
